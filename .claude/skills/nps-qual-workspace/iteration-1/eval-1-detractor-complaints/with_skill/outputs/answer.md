# Top Detractor Complaints — Sprint 14

**Data Source:** Sprint 14 survey responses | **Sample Size:** 241 detractors (scores 0–6) with feedback | **Method:** Pre-coded theme mapping + evocativeness scoring

---

## Theme Distribution

| Theme | Count | % of Total | Composition |
|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Slow Speed** | 48 | 19.9% | Buffering, low throughput, speed issues |
| **Frequent Disconnections** | 44 | 18.3% | Drops, "baar baar band", unstable connection |
| **General Negative** | 41 | 17.0% | Non-specific "bad", "bekar", poor service complaints |
| **Internet Down/Outage** | 11 | 4.6% | Complete outages, "net nahi chal raha" |
| **Complaint Resolution (Bad)** | 10 | 4.1% | Unresolved tickets, "complaint ka koi fayda nahi" |
| **28-Day Billing** | 6 | 2.5% | Calendar vs 28-day plan frustration |
| **Range/Coverage** | 5 | 2.1% | Weak signal, dead zones |
| **Call Centre (Bad)** | 4 | 1.7% | Can't reach support, unhelpful agents |
| **Pricing — Expensive** | 3 | 1.2% | "Mehnga", value concerns |
| **OTT/Content** | 3 | 1.2% | Limited streaming channels, content requests |
| **RDNI (Recharge → No Internet)** | 2 | 0.8% | Recharge done but net won't start |
| **Bad Technician/Partner** | 1 | 0.4% | Late, rude service personnel |
| **Unclassified** | 58 | 24.1% | Generic feedback, blank responses |

**Key Finding:** Speed and reliability issues dominate. 38.2% of coded detractors cite either slow speed or disconnections. Almost one-in-four responses are unclassified (low-signal feedback like "ok", "kutch nii", or blanks).

## Sentiment Highlights

**Pain Points in Customer Voice:**

1. **Broken promise of speed**: Customers explicitly compare Wiom's claimed speeds to competitors and feel misled. The phrase "2G ki tarah" (like 2G) is used repeatedly despite premium plans.

2. **Monthly recurring failures**: Patterns of network drops every 1–2 weeks, or consistently 10–20 days of monthly downtime, signal systemic reliability issues.

3. **Support fatigue**: Multiple customers mention calling 10–15 times for the same issue with no resolution. The phrase "complaint ka koi fayda nahi" captures abandonment of hope.

4. **Billing-to-service mismatch**: Two distinct pain points collide:
   - 28-day billing = perceived unfairness vs calendar month
   - RDNI (recharge done, net won't start) = money spent, service absent

5. **Location-based dead zones**: Range complaints suggest geographic inequality — some areas work, others don't.

6. **The competitor shadow**: "Airtel ka WiFi hai" and "should switch to another company" indicate active churn risk.

---

## Methodology Notes

- **Sample**: 241 detractors (NPS 0–6) with feedback out of total respondents
- **Evocativeness scoring formula**: `min(text_length, 300) + |score - 5| × 20` — prioritizes longer, more specific rants and extreme scores (0–2, 5–6 boundary cases)
- **Language preservation**: Hindi and Hinglish verbatims kept in original form for authenticity; English translations in parentheses for clarity
- **Unclassified (58 responses, 24%)**: Includes blanks, single-word answers ("ok", "kutch nii"), and low-signal feedback. Reflects real customer effort variance in qualitative surveys
