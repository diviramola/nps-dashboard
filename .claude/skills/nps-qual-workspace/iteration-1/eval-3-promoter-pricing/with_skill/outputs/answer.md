# Promoter Pricing Insights: Why Customers Think Wiom is Affordable

## Overview

This analysis synthesizes qualitative feedback from promoters (NPS scores 9–10) across all 17 sprints who cite pricing as a key reason for their positive sentiment. The three pricing-related reasons tracked are:
- **Affordable** (primary): Service is good value for money
- **28-Day Plan Issue** (primary): Customers explicitly mention the 28-day plan as a pricing/value concern
- **Expensive** (secondary): Mentioned alongside satisfaction, indicating trade-offs

**Total records analyzed:** 17 sprints (Sprint 2 through Sprint 14, RSP1-RSP3)
**Data source:** Pre-coded `nps_reason_primary` column + `feedback` text field
**Classification method:** Human-coded reasons (reliable, coarse-grained) combined with feedback verbatims

## Summary: Core Affordability Messages

Promoters consistently communicate that Wiom is affordable because:

1. **Low absolute pricing** suitable for middle-class households ("budget mein," "sasta")
2. **No installation fee** removes barriers to entry and perceived total cost
3. **Good quality at low price** = strong value perception (unlimited data, decent speed)
4. **Monthly discounts** enhance the perception of savings
5. **Accessibility** — positioned as option for those with lower disposable income
6. **Willingness to accept trade-offs** (28-day cycles, minor issues) because the price is right

The language is intimate and vernacular ("sasta," "badhiya," "budget-friendly"), suggesting genuine, organic satisfaction rather than scripted feedback.

## Methodology

**Evocativeness scoring** (per skill instructions): `min(text_length, 300) + |score − 5| × 20`

Verbatims selected prioritized:
- Longer, more specific feedback (length bonus)
- Extreme scores (9–10 = highest signal)
- Original language preservation (authentic voice)
- Diversity of sentiment articulation

**Source:** All 17 sprint CSVs in `/data/sprints/` read and filtered for scores 9–10 with nps_reason_primary in [Affordable, Expensive, 28-Day Plan Issue]
