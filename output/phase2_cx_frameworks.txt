======================================================================
PHASE 2 -- CX FRAMEWORKS REPORT
Applied to Wiom Home WiFi ISP
======================================================================

Company Context:
  - Indian home WiFi ISP targeting lower-middle-income households
  - 80% customer base in Delhi NCR, remainder in Mumbai and UP
  - Partner-based installation model (field technicians are partners, not employees)
  - 28-day billing cycle (not calendar month)
  - ~13K NPS respondents: 46.1% Promoters, 7.9% Passives, 46.0% Detractors
  - NPS Score: ~0 (net zero, bimodal distribution -- 42.6% score 10, 20.4% score 0)
  - 68% Hinglish comments, 21% English, 10% Hindi Devanagari
  - 30% first-time WiFi users (of those with known status)
  - Overall churn rate: ~24% among Loyal customers (270d+ tenure)
  - Detractor churn: 27.8% vs Promoter churn: 20.2%

Data Sources: Phase 0 Data Profile, Phase 1 Hindi NLP, Phase 1.3 Theme Discovery
Report Date: 2026-03-18

======================================================================
1. SERVQUAL FRAMEWORK FOR HOME INTERNET ISP
======================================================================

The SERVQUAL model measures the gap between customer expectations and
perceived service across five dimensions. Below is the Wiom-specific
application grounded in actual NPS verbatim data.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 TANGIBLES (Physical Facilities, Equipment, Appearance)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

What customers see and touch:

  Router/Netbox Quality:
  - Wiom provides a shared router (netbox) at no upfront cost to customers
  - Router issues (red light blinking, power failures, cable problems)
    affect 0.5% of comments but carry 38.5% churn -- the HIGHEST churn
    rate of any theme
  - Customers report: "Router me red light show ho rha h aaj tak koi
    nhi aaya banane" (red light on router, nobody came to fix for 10 days)
  - Device types tracked: GX, SY, TP routers -- TP-Link devices show
    lower NPS in Sprint 1 data (NPS: -11% vs GX at +5%)
  - Router is Wiom-owned property; threats of router recovery if recharge
    lapses create anxiety (see Section 2.5 Reverse Quality)

  App/Digital Interface:
  - Wiom app mentioned in 0.4% of comments, 85.7% negative sentiment
  - Issues: usage data not updating, recharge option missing, app crashes
  - "App me exact wifi usage show hi nahi karta" -- data transparency gap
  - Invoice download not available in-app, requiring calls for billing docs

  Network Infrastructure:
  - Fiber-based last-mile delivery through partner network
  - Optical power tracked per customer (column in sprint data)
  - Infrastructure not directly visible to customers but manifests through
    signal range and stability complaints

  SERVQUAL Gap Assessment (Tangibles):
  - Expected: Working, clean hardware with indicator lights showing status
  - Perceived: Unreliable hardware that sometimes fails without quick
    replacement; app that does not reflect real-time usage
  - Gap Size: MODERATE -- low frequency but catastrophic when it occurs
  - Priority: Fix router replacement SLA (currently highest churn driver)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.2 RELIABILITY (Ability to Perform Promised Service Dependably)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

This is Wiom's most critical SERVQUAL dimension. Three of the top five
NPS themes relate to reliability failures.

  Uptime Consistency:
  - Disconnection frequency: 12.3% of classified comments, 75.4% from
    Detractors, mean NPS = 3.4, churn = 17.0%
  - Internet down/outage: 2.7% of comments, 83.0% from Detractors,
    mean NPS = 2.4, churn = 19.7%
  - Customers report "hafte mein ek baar kharab ho jata hai" (breaks
    once a week) and "15 din se internet band hai" (internet down 15 days)
  - Disconnection + outage themes overlap in 127 comments, indicating
    chronic instability for a subset of users

  Speed Delivery:
  - Slow speed: 16.0% of classified comments (largest single theme),
    61.9% from Detractors, mean NPS = 4.6, churn = 17.8%
  - Promise vs delivery gap: "Starting me bola tha 100 mbps speed hogi
    but aaati 5 ya 6 hi h" (promised 100 Mbps, getting 5-6 Mbps)
  - Speed complaints have a 43% positive sentiment rate, suggesting many
    customers find speed acceptable MOST of the time but experience
    periodic degradation
  - P40, P50, P90 speed metrics tracked internally (separate analysis tab)

  Billing Accuracy:
  - 28-day billing cycle is the single most emotionally charged issue
  - 1.0% of comments explicitly mention it, but 73.7% of those are
    Detractors with mean NPS = 3.0 and churn = 31.6%
  - RDNI (Recharge Done, No Internet): 0.1% of comments, 80% Detractors,
    churn = 40.0% -- payment taken but service not delivered

  Plan Activation Post-Recharge:
  - Autopay issues: 0.3% of comments, 86.7% Detractors, mean NPS = 1.8
  - "Auto payment ke baad bhi bar bar call aate hai payment kar diya kya"
    -- even after autopay, harassment calls continue
  - "Recharge ke baad net nahi chala" -- service does not activate after
    payment, violating the most basic reliability promise

  SERVQUAL Gap Assessment (Reliability):
  - Expected: Internet works continuously after recharge; promised speed
    is delivered; billing matches calendar month expectations
  - Perceived: Frequent disconnections; speed far below promises; 28-day
    cycle feels like overcharging; recharge sometimes does not activate
  - Gap Size: LARGE -- reliability failures are the #1 volume driver
    of Detractor sentiment
  - Priority: HIGHEST. Must close speed/stability gap before any other
    improvement can move NPS

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.3 RESPONSIVENESS (Willingness to Help, Prompt Service)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Complaint Resolution TAT:
  - Bad complaint resolution: 2.9% of comments, 85.1% Detractors,
    mean NPS = 2.4, churn = 20.5%
  - Customers report 48-hour+ resolution times: "complaint karte hain
    to 48 ghante mein service milati hai"
  - Good complaint resolution: only 0.2% of comments, but mean NPS = 9.2
    and those customers say "within 10-15 min problem solve kr dete h"
  - The gap between best-case (15 min) and typical-case (48h+) resolution
    suggests inconsistency, not systemic inability

  Technician Dispatch Speed:
  - Bad technician experience: 0.3% of comments, 70.6% Detractors,
    mean NPS = 3.4, churn = 23.5%
  - Partner model creates accountability gaps: "technicians bolte h ate
    hi ni h location pe" (technicians say they are coming but do not)
  - Shifting/relocation requests take 12+ days: "We have requested for
    shifting but we have given a time period of 12 days"
  - Shifting theme has 66.7% churn -- effectively a termination event

  Call Center Pickup:
  - Bad call center: 0.4% of comments, 57.1% Detractors, churn = 38.1%
  - "Customer care call nahi uthata" (call center does not pick up)
  - "Aap kisi or number se customer care ko call nahi kar skte" --
    restricted to registered number, creating friction for family members
  - Good call center: 0.2% of comments, but still only 44.4% Promoters
    (even positive mentions carry mixed sentiment)

  SERVQUAL Gap Assessment (Responsiveness):
  - Expected: Quick acknowledgment, same-day resolution for basic issues,
    technician visit within 24 hours for hardware issues
  - Perceived: Multi-day resolution cycles; technician no-shows; call
    center unreachable; no proactive communication during outages
  - Gap Size: LARGE -- second only to reliability in NPS impact
  - Priority: HIGH. Fast resolution can partially compensate for
    reliability failures

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.4 ASSURANCE (Knowledge, Courtesy, Trust-Building)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Pricing Transparency:
  - 28-day billing is perceived as deceptive: "ये तो उपभोक्ताओं के
    साथ सीधा धोखा है" (this is outright fraud against consumers)
  - Customers calculate the hidden cost: "According to you 24 day extra
    charges received in year" (customer computed 24 extra days/year)
  - Price increases without corresponding value: "apne 449 se 500 to
    kar diya" (you raised from 449 to 500)
  - Pricing perception split: 2.0% mention affordability positively
    (mean NPS 8.6) vs 0.8% find it expensive (mean NPS 5.3)

  Service Guarantees:
  - No formal SLA communicated to customers for uptime or resolution
  - Lack of proactive outage notifications: "No update, no intimation.
    Why should customers raise tickets every time?"
  - Competitor comparison: "You should inform users when service is down,
    like @airtelindia does"
  - No plan hold option during extended outages: "plan hold karna ka
    bhi option nhi hai"

  Data Security/Privacy:
  - App security concern raised: "es app me bilkul bhi security nhi"
    (this app has no security at all)
  - Phone number privacy noted in Google Form surveys ("phone number
    goptaniy rakha jayega" -- phone number will be kept confidential)
  - Low salience overall -- security is not a top-of-mind concern for
    this customer segment compared to basic service reliability

  SERVQUAL Gap Assessment (Assurance):
  - Expected: Honest pricing (calendar month = 30 days), clear service
    guarantees, proactive communication
  - Perceived: 28-day billing feels dishonest; no guarantees; no
    proactive outage communication
  - Gap Size: MODERATE-HIGH -- concentrated in billing perception
  - Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH. 28-day issue has outsized emotional impact
    relative to its mention frequency

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.5 EMPATHY (Caring, Individualized Attention)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Hindi-First Support:
  - 68.3% of NPS comments are in Hinglish, 10% in Hindi Devanagari
  - NPS survey questions are bilingual (Hindi + English)
  - Support interactions appear to happen in Hindi/Hinglish based on
    customer quotes about call center conversations
  - This is a baseline met -- not a differentiator but table stakes

  Understanding Financial Constraints:
  - Target segment is lower-middle-income; affordability is the #1
    positive driver (mean NPS 8.6, lowest churn at 6.4%)
  - "Wiom internet middle class logo ke liye bhot acha hai or budget
    me bhi hai" (Wiom is great for middle class, fits the budget)
  - 30% are first-time WiFi users -- digital inclusion is real
  - Yet: "jinko financial koi problem h wo yah use kr sakta h" (those
    with financial problems can use this) -- positioning as fallback,
    not aspiration
  - Price sensitivity means even Rs. 20 coupon value is noticed: "jyada
    se jyada 20 rs ka coupon milta hai 20rs main kya hota h"

  Flexible Payment:
  - Cash and online payment modes tracked (column in sprint data)
  - Autopay available but unreliable (see 1.2 Reliability)
  - No EMI or split-payment option visible in data
  - No plan hold during financial hardship -- missed recharge triggers
    router recovery threats

  SERVQUAL Gap Assessment (Empathy):
  - Expected: Flexibility during financial difficulties, understanding
    that every rupee matters, patient support in local language
  - Perceived: Language support adequate; pricing affordable but 28-day
    cycle and router threats feel coercive; no financial flexibility
  - Gap Size: MODERATE -- language is right but policies feel rigid
  - Priority: MEDIUM. Introduce pause/hold mechanisms

----------------------------------------------------------------------
1.6 SERVQUAL SUMMARY: WIOM GAP PRIORITIZATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Dimension       | Gap Size     | NPS Impact | Churn Impact | Priority
  ----------------+--------------+------------+--------------+---------
  Reliability     | LARGE        | HIGHEST    | HIGH         | 1
  Responsiveness  | LARGE        | HIGH       | HIGH         | 2
  Assurance       | MODERATE-HIGH| MODERATE   | HIGH (28-day)| 3
  Tangibles       | MODERATE     | LOW (freq) | HIGHEST (per)| 4
  Empathy         | MODERATE     | LOW-MOD    | MODERATE     | 5

  Key Insight: Reliability and Responsiveness together account for
  ~58% of all classified Detractor comments. Closing these two gaps
  would shift the largest volume of customers toward Promoter territory.

2. KANO MODEL FOR HOME INTERNET
======================================================================

The Kano model classifies features by their relationship to customer
satisfaction. For Wiom's lower-middle-income segment:

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 MUST-BE QUALITIES (Expected, Absence Causes Dissatisfaction)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

These features do NOT increase satisfaction when present -- they only
cause dissatisfaction when absent. Their absence is catastrophic.

  1. Internet works after recharge
     - RDNI (Recharge Done No Internet) has 40% churn, mean NPS 1.4
     - Violating this is the single most damaging service failure
     - "Recharge ke baad net nahi chala" -- breaks the fundamental
       transactional promise

  2. Router powers on and stays on
     - Router device issues: 38.5% churn, mean NPS 3.1
     - "Red light blink problem" -- customer cannot self-diagnose
     - Hardware failure creates complete service blackout

  3. Basic connectivity (internet connects to devices)
     - General negative theme (unspecified issues): 27.3% churn
     - "Internet kaam hi nahi karta" (internet does not work at all)
     - Below this threshold, nothing else matters

  4. Recharge/payment can be completed
     - Autopay failures: 86.7% Detractors, mean NPS 1.8
     - "App me recharge option nahi milta" (recharge option missing in app)
     - Blocking the payment = blocking the service

  5. Service available in customer's area after installation
     - Shifting/relocation: 66.7% churn, 100% Detractors
     - Once installed, service must remain available at that location

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 ONE-DIMENSIONAL QUALITIES (More = Better, Linear Satisfaction)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Satisfaction scales proportionally with performance. These are the
battleground features where improvement directly lifts NPS.

  1. Download/upload speed
     - Largest theme by volume (16.0% of classified comments)
     - Speed satisfaction is linear: fast = NPS 8.5, slow = NPS 4.6
     - "Good speed" theme: 80.6% Promoters vs "slow speed": 61.9%
       Detractors
     - Every Mbps improvement moves the needle

  2. Uptime percentage (stability)
     - Disconnection frequency: 75.4% Detractors when mentioned
     - Good connectivity correlates with general positive sentiment
       (94% positive, NPS 8.1)
     - Not binary (Must-Be) but graduated -- customers tolerate
       occasional drops but not weekly disruptions

  3. Complaint resolution speed
     - Bad resolution: NPS 2.4; Good resolution: NPS 9.2
     - Linear relationship: 15-minute fixes create promoters, 48-hour
       fixes create detractors
     - "Within 10-15 min problem solve kr dete h" vs "5 din se internet
       band hai solution nahi kar rahe hai"

  4. WiFi signal range (coverage within home)
     - Range complaints: 3.1% of comments, 60.9% Detractors
     - "Ek room se doosre room tak nahi ja pati hai" (signal does not
       reach from one room to another)
     - More range = more satisfaction, linearly

  5. Price-to-value ratio
     - Affordable perception: NPS 8.6, 6.4% churn
     - Expensive perception: NPS 5.3, 28.9% churn
     - Value for money is relative to delivered quality

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 ATTRACTIVE QUALITIES (Delighters, Unexpected Positives)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

These create disproportionate satisfaction when present but do NOT
cause dissatisfaction when absent.

  1. Coupons/rewards/discounts
     - Mentioned positively: "hamen coupons bhi milte hain, itna sab
       kuchh kaun deta hai" (we get coupons too, who gives all this)
     - Discount on autopay recharge mentioned as positive surprise
     - Low absolute value (Rs. 20) but high perceived value for segment

  2. Free WiFi roaming (connect from any Wiom router)
     - "Additional benefit of wifi connect from any wiom router"
     - Unique feature vs competitors -- portable WiFi access
     - Not expected, so creates delight when discovered

  3. Proactive outage alerts
     - Currently ABSENT -- but would delight if implemented
     - Customer explicitly asked for it: "You should inform users when
       service is down, like @airtelindia does"
     - Would reduce call center load AND increase trust simultaneously

  4. OTT/streaming content access
     - 0.4% of comments mention OTT; currently a gap vs JioFiber
     - "500 me agar tv+ ott hona chahiye tha to or bhi behtr hota"
     - Would be attractive at current price points
     - Risk: could become One-Dimensional if competitors make it standard

  5. No upfront hardware cost (free router)
     - "Ye free lag jaata hai bas recharge ke paise dene hote hai"
     - "No initial big amount process" -- cited as reason for high NPS
     - Removes adoption barrier for lower-income households

  6. Referral program
     - Promoters actively recommend: "apne YouTube channel par recommend
       bhi kiya" and "apne doshton ko wiom wifi internet lagwane"
     - Formalizing referral rewards would amplify organic advocacy

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 INDIFFERENT QUALITIES (No Impact on Satisfaction)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

These features neither increase nor decrease satisfaction regardless
of their presence. Investment here yields no NPS return.

  1. Brand aesthetics/visual identity
     - Not mentioned in any of 5,507 customer comments
     - Customer segment cares about function, not brand polish
     - Generic "good" tags in Wiom's coding include aesthetics but
       customers never mention it

  2. Marketing messages/campaigns
     - No comments reference Wiom advertising or marketing
     - Customers evaluate based on lived experience only
     - Pre-purchase marketing is irrelevant to post-purchase NPS

  3. App visual design (vs functionality)
     - When app is mentioned, it is always about function (crashes,
       data not updating) never about UI appearance
     - "App me recharge option nahi milta" -- function, not design

  4. Multiple recharge plan tiers (variety)
     - "Different types of internet packs are not available" -- mentioned
       by exactly 1 customer in sample data
     - Current single-plan simplicity may actually be a strength for
       this segment

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 REVERSE QUALITIES (Presence Causes Dissatisfaction)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

These features REDUCE satisfaction. They are things Wiom does that
customers actively resent.

  1. 28-day billing cycle (vs expected 30-day calendar month)
     - 73.7% Detractors among those who mention it, churn = 31.6%
     - Perceived as deceptive: "ये तो उपभोक्ताओं के साथ सीधा धोखा है"
     - Customers calculate the annual overcharge: 13 billing cycles
       per year instead of 12 = extra month's payment
     - Even Promoters (NPS 10) complain: "aap chahte to 28 days 30
       days kar sakte hana taki customer acha lagta"
     - This is the clearest Reverse Quality in the data

  2. Hidden charges / unexpected costs
     - Price increases without notice: "449 se 500 to kar diya"
     - "Koi extra charges nhi" is cited as a POSITIVE -- implying
       that extra charges elsewhere are a known negative
     - Any opacity in billing becomes a reverse quality for this
       price-sensitive segment

  3. Router return threats upon non-recharge
     - "1 month richarge nhi krte to router mang lete h ek dum se"
       (if you miss one month recharge, they suddenly demand the router)
     - Creates anxiety and coercive relationship
     - Transforms the "free router" attractive quality into a
       threatening liability

  4. Harassment calls for recharge
     - "Bar bar presan karte hai auto payment ke bad bi bar bar call
       aate hai payment kar diya kya" (they harass even after autopay)
     - Aggressive collection behavior on a Rs. 500 product damages
       trust disproportionately

----------------------------------------------------------------------
2.6 KANO CATEGORY SUMMARY
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Category       | Features                  | NPS Impact    | Action
  ---------------+---------------------------+---------------+---------
  Must-Be        | Post-recharge activation, | No upside,    | Monitor
                 | router function, basic    | catastrophic  | and
                 | connectivity, payment     | downside      | maintain

  ---------------+---------------------------+---------------+---------

                 | app visual design, plan   |               | invest
                 | variety                   |               |
  ---------------+---------------------------+---------------+---------

                 | charges, router threats,  | destroys      | or
                 | harassment calls          | satisfaction  | redesign

3. CUSTOMER EFFORT SCORE (CES) MAPPING
======================================================================

CES measures how much effort customers must expend to get value from
the service. Lower effort = higher loyalty. Mapped to Wiom touchpoints:

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 LOW EFFORT TOUCHPOINTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Initial Installation
     - Partner-based model means customer does not need to self-install
     - "Jaldi installation ho gaya" (quick installation)
     - No upfront cost reduces decision effort
     - Effort Level: LOW
     - NPS Implication: Positive, but rarely mentioned (table stakes)

  2. Routine Internet Usage (when working)
     - When service is stable, effort is zero -- WiFi is background
     - "24 ghante service, fast speed" -- it just works
     - No configuration or management needed by customer
     - Effort Level: VERY LOW
     - NPS Implication: Drives general_positive theme (18.2% of comments)

  3. Basic Recharge (online/UPI)
     - "Easy recharge method" -- UPI integration noted positively
     - Simple recurring transaction
     - Effort Level: LOW
     - NPS Implication: Neutral -- expected to work

  4. WiFi Roaming (connecting at other Wiom locations)
     - Automatic connection to other Wiom routers in range
     - "Additional benefit of wifi connect from any wiom router"
     - Effort Level: VERY LOW (automatic)
     - NPS Implication: Delighter (Attractive quality)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2 HIGH EFFORT TOUCHPOINTS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Complaint Registration and Follow-Up
     - Must call customer care from registered number only
     - "Aap kisi or number se customer care ko call nahi kar skte"
     - Call center often unreachable: "customer care call nahi uthata"
     - Repeated follow-up required: "bar bar customer support ko bolna
       pdta h" (have to tell customer support again and again)
     - Effort Level: VERY HIGH
     - NPS Implication: complaint_resolution_bad = NPS 2.4, churn 20.5%

  2. Outage Self-Diagnosis and Resolution
     - No proactive alerts -- customer discovers outage themselves
     - Must figure out if problem is router, network, or area-wide
     - "Red light blink" -- customer cannot interpret device indicators
     - Must initiate contact with Wiom (high effort to even start)
     - Effort Level: VERY HIGH
     - NPS Implication: internet_down_outage = NPS 2.4, churn 19.7%

  3. Getting Invoice/Billing Documentation
     - "Invoice which is not downloadable we have to call everytime"
     - No self-serve billing portal
     - Effort Level: HIGH
     - NPS Implication: Low frequency but impacts trust/assurance

  4. Shifting/Relocation of Connection
     - 12+ day timeline for a shifting request
     - 66.7% churn -- most customers give up
     - "Shifting not feasible" coded as a reason category
     - Effort Level: EXTREMELY HIGH (often impossible)
     - NPS Implication: Effectively a termination event

  5. Escalation Beyond First-Level Support
     - "Solution received after your Delhi office involved" -- had to
       escalate to HQ for a router replacement
     - No clear escalation path visible to customers
     - Effort Level: HIGH
     - NPS Implication: Delays compound frustration exponentially

  6. Recharge During Service Interruption
     - If service has lapsed, pressure to recharge immediately
     - "Sudden karna padta h recharge" (have to recharge suddenly)
     - No grace period or plan hold option
     - Effort Level: MODERATE-HIGH (psychological pressure)
     - NPS Implication: Creates coercive dynamic

  7. Autopay Setup and Management
     - Autopay does not always work: payment deducted but service not
       activated
     - "Auto payment is enabled [but] I have to make payment manually"
     - Effort Level: HIGH (when it fails, defeating the purpose)
     - NPS Implication: autopay_payment_mode = NPS 1.8

----------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 CES IMPLICATIONS BY JOURNEY STAGE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Journey Stage         | Avg Effort | Key Friction               | Impact
  ----------------------+------------+----------------------------+--------
  Booking/Discovery     | LOW        | Minimal -- partner handles | Neutral
  Installation          | LOW        | Quick, free installation   | Positive
  Free Trial            | LOW        | Service works, exploring   | Positive
  First Recharge        | LOW-MOD    | Learning payment process   | Neutral
  Ongoing Usage         | VERY LOW   | Invisible when working     | Positive
  Complaint Filing      | VERY HIGH  | Unreachable call center    | Negative
  Outage Experience     | VERY HIGH  | No alerts, self-diagnosis  | Negative
  Recharge Renewal      | LOW-MOD    | 28-day cycle confusion     | Negative
  Billing/Invoice       | HIGH       | No self-serve portal       | Negative
  Shifting/Relocation   | EXTREME    | 12+ day timeline           | Churn
  Churn Decision        | LOW        | Just stop recharging       | --

  Critical Insight: The effort curve is U-shaped. Onboarding is low
  effort, steady usage is low effort, but any EXCEPTION (complaint,
  outage, relocation) requires disproportionately high effort. This
  creates a "works until it doesn't" experience where customers feel
  trapped when problems arise.

  CES Priority: Reduce effort at complaint, outage, and billing
  touchpoints. These are where customers are ALREADY upset -- adding
  effort on top of a problem multiplies dissatisfaction.

4. JOBS-TO-BE-DONE (JTBD) FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

What "job" are Wiom customers "hiring" home WiFi to accomplish?

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4.1 FUNCTIONAL JOBS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Enable children's education (online classes, homework, research)
     - "I am a student he must help me for online study"
     - Education is the primary justification for internet expenditure
       in lower-middle-income households
     - Outages during online classes create acute frustration
     - Job Metric: Uptime during 9am-3pm (school hours)
     - Failure Mode: Disconnection during exams or live classes

  2. Access entertainment (streaming, social media, gaming)
     - "YouTube", "Netflix" mentioned in speed context
     - "Jab game play karta hai wrost network" (worst network when gaming)
     - Entertainment is the high-bandwidth demand driver
     - Job Metric: Sustained streaming-grade speed (5+ Mbps)
     - Failure Mode: Buffering, slow downloads

  3. Support work-from-home (WFH)
     - "Bhot preshani hoti h work from home krne me" (WFH creates
       problems with this internet)
     - "Agar meeting bgera hoti ha to cancel ho jati ha" (meetings
       get cancelled)
     - WFH increasingly common post-COVID even in this segment
     - Job Metric: Stable upload speed for video calls, low latency
     - Failure Mode: Drops during video meetings

  4. Enable family communication (WhatsApp, video calls)
     - Base-level internet job for the segment
     - WhatsApp works even on poor connections, so this job is usually
       fulfilled
     - Job Metric: Basic connectivity (any speed)
     - Failure Mode: Complete outage only

  5. Replace expensive mobile data
     - "Mobile mein bina data wale recharge plan bahut saste nahi hote"
       (mobile data-less recharge plans are not cheap)
     - WiFi hired specifically to offload mobile data costs
     - Job Metric: Cost per GB lower than mobile data
     - Failure Mode: When WiFi is down, mobile data cost resumes

----------------------------------------------------------------------
4.2 EMOTIONAL JOBS
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Feel connected and not left behind
     - 30% are first-time WiFi users -- internet access is a step
       toward digital inclusion
     - "Har koi lagwa sakta hai...sabse badi khaasiyat yahi hai"
       (anyone can get it...that is the best feature)
     - Emotional Job: Not being digitally excluded from modern life

  2. Feel like a good provider for family
     - WiFi is a household investment justified by children's education
     - "Middle class logo ke liye bhot acha hai" (great for middle class)
     - Emotional Job: Providing opportunities for family advancement

  3. Feel secure and not cheated
     - 28-day billing triggers "dhoka" (fraud) language
     - "Sab kuch free of cost hai koi extra charges nhi" -- the
       ABSENCE of hidden charges is explicitly celebrated
     - Emotional Job: Trust that the provider is honest and fair

7. TENURE-DEPENDENT JOURNEY EMPHASIS
======================================================================

What matters most at each stage of the customer lifecycle:

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 ONBOARDING (0-15 days) | n=25, Mean NPS=7.2, Median=8.0
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  What Matters Most:
  - First impression of speed and connectivity
  - Router placement quality (determines range for entire relationship)
  - App setup and first recharge experience
  - Understanding billing cycle and plan details

  Key Frameworks:
  - Kano Must-Be: Does internet work out of the box? (Pass/Fail)
  - JTBD: Validating the decision to switch from mobile data
  - CES: Should be zero effort -- partner handles everything

  Critical Metrics:
  - Time from install to first successful connection (minutes)
  - Speed achieved in first 24 hours vs plan promise
  - First recharge conversion rate (trial -> paid)

  Risk Factors:
  - 46.7% negative sentiment in Onboarding -- concerning given n=25
  - 60% comment fill rate (highest of any bucket) -- new users are
    vocal, both positively and negatively
  - If first experience is bad, customer never returns to Promoter

  Priority Actions:
  - Ensure router placement optimizes range at installation
  - Speed test at installation with customer present
  - Clear explanation of 28-day billing before first recharge
  - 48-hour follow-up call/message to check satisfaction

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7.2 EARLY LIFE (16-60 days) | n=125, Mean NPS=6.4, Median=9.0
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  What Matters Most:
  - Consistency of experience after honeymoon period
  - First complaint experience (if any issue arises)
  - First recharge cycle completion (understanding 28-day billing)
  - Speed stability across different times of day

  Key Frameworks:
  - Kano One-Dimensional: Speed and stability now measured daily
  - SERVQUAL Reliability: "Does it keep working like day 1?"
  - CES: First complaint experience sets long-term expectation

  Critical Metrics:
  - Uptime % in first 60 days
  - Number of complaints in first 60 days
  - Second recharge conversion rate (first cycle -> second cycle)
  - Speed degradation from week 1 to week 4

  Risk Factors:
  - 37.7% negative sentiment (better than onboarding)
  - Median NPS of 9.0 but mean of 6.4 -- highly bimodal
  - First billing cycle is make-or-break for 28-day perception
  - 0% churn recorded (sample bias -- too early for churn signal)

  Priority Actions:
  - Proactive check-in before first recharge date
  - Explain 28-day cycle clearly with next due date
  - Monitor speed delivery vs plan promise in first 30 days
  - Fast-track any Early Life complaints (golden period for recovery)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7.3 ESTABLISHING (61-120 days) | n=108, Mean NPS=6.1, Median=7.5
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  What Matters Most:
  - Service consistency under varying conditions (weather, load)
  - Value perception after 3-4 billing cycles (price fatigue?)
  - Competitor awareness and comparison
  - Accumulating small frustrations (range issues, speed dips)

  Key Frameworks:
  - SERVQUAL Assurance: Trust in pricing fairness solidifies here
  - Kano Attractive: Coupons and rewards have most impact at this stage
    (retention tool during evaluation window)
  - JTBD Emotional: "Am I getting what I'm paying for?"

  Critical Metrics:
  - Consecutive on-time recharges (loyalty signal)
  - Complaint frequency trend (increasing = risk)
  - Competitor mentions in NPS comments (20 comments total)
  - Speed consistency score (variance, not just average)

  Risk Factors:
  - NPS declining from 7.2 (Onboarding) to 6.1 (Establishing)
  - 28.8% negative sentiment (better than loyal, interestingly)
  - This is the "evaluation" window where customers actively compare
  - 0% churn recorded (still too early for full cycle data)

  Priority Actions:
  - Targeted coupon/discount at 90-day mark (retention incentive)
  - Speed audit at 60-day mark to catch degradation
  - Competitor pre-emption messaging if JioFiber/Airtel available
  - Celebrate "3 months with Wiom" milestone in app

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7.4 STEADY STATE (121-270 days) | n=186, Mean NPS=5.7, Median=7.0
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  What Matters Most:
  - Chronic issue resolution (recurring problems now entrenched)
  - Price/value re-evaluation (6-9 months of paying Rs. 500/cycle)
  - Support experience accumulation (every interaction remembered)
  - Network infrastructure quality in their specific location

  Key Frameworks:
  - SERVQUAL Responsiveness: By now, customer has likely needed support
    at least once. That experience defines their NPS
  - Kano Reverse: 28-day billing irritation COMPOUNDS over time (customer
    has now "lost" ~12-18 extra days cumulatively)
  - CES: Effort memories become crystallized opinions

  Critical Metrics:
  - Total tickets raised in last 3 months (tracked in sprint data)
  - Recharge regularity (gaps = churn intent)
  - Optical power readings (network health proxy)
  - NPS trend vs previous survey (improving or declining?)

  Risk Factors:
  - NPS continues declining: 5.7 mean (lowest of any bucket)
  - 43.1% negative sentiment
  - Customers now have clear reference points for comparison
  - 0% churn recorded (sample may not capture full lifecycle)

  Priority Actions:
  - Proactive health check for customers with >2 tickets in 90 days
  - Loyalty recognition (6-month milestone, small reward)
  - Address chronic issues with targeted infrastructure fixes
  - Survey-triggered intervention for Detractors (closed-loop program)

----------------------------------------------------------------------
7.5 LOYAL (270+ days) | n=12,601, Mean NPS=5.9, Median=8.0
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  What Matters Most:
  - Sustained service quality (no degradation over time)
  - Feeling valued as a long-term customer (not taken for granted)
  - Price competitiveness as market evolves
  - Cumulative trust vs cumulative frustration balance

  Key Frameworks:
  - JTBD Social: These are the customers who recommend (or warn against)
    Wiom in their communities -- their NPS IS the growth engine
  - SERVQUAL Empathy: Long-tenure customers expect recognition
  - Kano One-Dimensional: All dimensions now fully experienced and
    evaluated; no more surprises

  Critical Metrics:
  - Churn rate: 24.3% for this bucket (highest because lifecycle
    exposure allows churn to manifest)
  - Referral activity (YouTube mentions, community recommendations)
  - Recharge consistency (monthly pattern)
  - Cumulative ticket history vs NPS score

  Risk Factors:
  - 48.4% negative sentiment (highest of any tenure bucket)
  - 96.6% of the sample is in this bucket -- this IS Wiom's customer base
  - Bimodal distribution: median 8.0 but mean 5.9 -- many 0s AND 10s
  - Competitor switching most likely here (sunk cost is zero)
  - Billing_28day theme is 69.8% concentrated in 6+ month tenure --
    the irritation GROWS over time

  Priority Actions:
  - Loyalty program with meaningful benefits (not Rs. 20 coupons)
  - Annual price review communication (transparent pricing)
  - Priority support queue for 12+ month customers
  - Win-back program for churned loyal customers (27.8% of Detractors,
    20.2% of Promoters -- even Promoters churn)
  - Closed-loop NPS program: call every Detractor within 48 hours

7.6 TENURE JOURNEY SUMMARY

  Tenure Stage    | Mean NPS | Key Focus          | Biggest Risk
  ----------------+----------+--------------------+---------------------
  Onboarding      |   7.2    | First impression   | Bad install/speed
  Early Life      |   6.4    | Consistency        | First billing shock

  where initial excitement erodes as reality sets in.

  Strategic Implication: The critical intervention window is 60-180 days.
  Customers who survive this period with positive NPS become long-term
  assets. Those who don't are either churning or becoming vocal Detractors.

8.3 WIOM'S POSITION IN CONTEXT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Relative Position:

  - ABOVE BSNL and poor-performing regional ISPs

  - Bimodal distribution (42.6% score 10, 20.4% score 0) is unusual
  - Most ISPs have a normal-ish distribution with a right or left skew
  - Wiom's bimodality suggests two distinct experience cohorts:
    (a) Customers with stable, well-installed connections = Promoters
    (b) Customers with infrastructure/partner issues = Detractors
  - This is NOT a universal service problem -- it is a CONSISTENCY problem

  What "Good" Would Look Like:
  - Target NPS: +20 to +30 (achievable with current Promoter base)
  - Required: Convert ~15-20% of Detractors to Passives or Promoters
  - Translates to: ~900-1,200 customers moving from 0-6 to 7+ scores
  - Primary levers: Fix disconnection frequency and slow speed for the
    bottom quartile of network performance

  Benchmark by Churn Rate:
  - Wiom overall churn: ~24% (Loyal bucket)
  - Indian ISP industry average churn: 15-25% annually
  - Budget segment churn: typically 25-35%
  - Wiom is within range but at the higher end

  - Wiom Detractor churn: 27.8%
  - Industry Detractor churn: typically 30-45%
  - Wiom Promoter churn: 20.2%
  - Industry Promoter churn: typically 5-12%
  - CRITICAL: Wiom's Promoter churn (20.2%) is ABNORMALLY HIGH
  - This suggests external factors (relocations, financial constraints,
    competitor entry) are churning even satisfied customers

     - Benchmark: Even budget ISPs in India achieve <35% Detractor share

     - Focus: loyalty program, competitive pre-emption, shifting support
     - Benchmark: ISPs with good retention hold Promoter churn at 5-10%

  3. Increase Passive share from 8% to 12-15% (absorb from Detractors)
     - Focus: partial fixes that move scores from 0-5 to 6-8
     - Benchmark: Healthy ISP distributions have 15-20% Passives

     - Benchmark: ISPs with closed-loop programs see +5 to +10 NPS
