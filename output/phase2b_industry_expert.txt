1. ISP EXPERIENCE DRIVER TAXONOMY
======================================================================

What objectively determines home internet satisfaction, ranked by
impact for Wiom's demographic (lower-middle-income, Hindi-first,
80% Delhi NCR, last-mile WiFi, partner-based delivery):

--- 1.1 RELIABILITY DRIVERS (Highest Impact) ---

Connection Stability (Disconnection Frequency)
  - For Wiom's base, this is likely THE #1 driver
  - WiFi over last-mile radio is inherently less stable than fiber
  - Customers describe: "bar bar band hota hai" (keeps going off)
  - Acceptable threshold: <2 noticeable drops per day
  - Danger threshold: >5 drops per day -> Detractor territory
  - Key insight: SHORT drops (5-10 sec) are more disruptive than
    one long outage because they interrupt active use (video calls,
    streaming, gaming)

Uptime Consistency
  - Industry standard for home internet: 99.5% monthly uptime
  - For Wiom's infrastructure: 95%+ is likely the realistic target
  - BUT: Monthly uptime masks peak-hour degradation
  - 95% monthly = ~36 hours of downtime/month = unacceptable
  - 99% monthly = ~7 hours/month = borderline acceptable
  - Critical: Downtime during 7-11 PM (prime time) weighs 3-5x more
    than 2-6 AM downtime in customer perception

Recovery Speed After Outages
  - Industry best: <30 minutes to restore service
  - Customer tolerance: 2-4 hours before serious frustration
  - After 24 hours without internet: churn consideration begins
  - Key: Communication during outage matters more than speed
  - "We know it's down, working on it" reduces churn risk 40%+

--- 1.2 SPEED DELIVERY DRIVERS ---

Advertised vs Actual Speed Gap
  - Industry norm: deliver 80%+ of advertised speed consistently
  - Common ISP trap: advertise "up to 100 Mbps" but deliver 15 Mbps
  - For Wiom's segment: absolute speed matters less than sufficiency
  - Key use cases and minimum viable speed:
    * WhatsApp video call: 1.5 Mbps
    * HD YouTube/streaming: 5 Mbps
    * Online education: 3 Mbps
    * Basic browsing: 1 Mbps
    * Multiple devices (3-5): multiply by device count
  - Wiom customers likely need 10-25 Mbps reliably, not 100+ Mbps

Peak-Hour Degradation
  - 6-11 PM is when families use internet most heavily
  - Shared infrastructure = bandwidth contention during peak
  - Customer says "slow" but really means "slow at 8 PM when I
    actually need it, even though speed test at 2 AM is fine"
  - Solution: partner-level bandwidth management, not just NAS uptime

Consistency Over Speed
  - Jitter and packet loss cause more perceived quality problems
    than raw bandwidth for real-time applications
  - A stable 10 Mbps >> a fluctuating 5-50 Mbps connection
  - Wiom likely measures throughput (data_usage_okr) but NOT
    consistency metrics (jitter, latency, packet loss)

--- 1.3 VALUE PERCEPTION DRIVERS ---

Price-to-Quality Ratio vs Alternatives
  - JioFiber: Rs 399/month for 30 Mbps, 30-day cycle, fiber
  - Airtel Xstream: Rs 499/month, fiber, bundled OTT
  - Wiom: ~Rs 449-565/month, WiFi (not fiber), 28-day cycle
  - The 28-day billing is a significant competitive disadvantage:
    * Customer pays for 13 months/year vs 12 for competitors
    * Effective annual premium: ~Rs 500-600/year (one extra month)
    * This is noticed and resented, especially by price-sensitive base

Switching Cost Assessment
  - Wiom has LOW switching costs: no contract, free router (returned)
  - Competitors have HIGHER switching costs: installation fee,
    contracts, equipment deposits
  - Low switching cost = every dissatisfaction event is a potential
    churn trigger
  - Implication: Wiom must work HARDER on satisfaction because
    customers face no barrier to leaving

Hidden Cost Sensitivity
  - 28-day vs 30-day (hidden extra month cost)
  - Recharge failure -> days without internet (money + service lost)
  - Cash recharge premium (if any)
  - No auto-pause when plan expires (internet dies immediately)

--- 1.4 SUPPORT EXPERIENCE DRIVERS ---

First-Call Resolution Rate
  - Industry best-in-class: 70-80% FCR
  - Average ISP: 50-60% FCR
  - For Wiom: Unknown but likely low given "complaint karo to
    koi response nahi" theme
  - Each additional call doubles churn probability

Technician/Partner Quality
  - For Wiom this is UNIQUE: partners ARE the brand
  - Bad partner experience = bad Wiom experience (no separation)
  - Partner behaviors that destroy NPS:
    * Not showing up for scheduled visits
    * Rude or dismissive behavior
    * Taking too long to resolve issues
    * Pressuring for router return when customer delays recharge
  - Partner behaviors that create promoters:
    * Proactive outage communication
    * Quick response to complaints
    * Professional installation with speed test verification

--- 1.5 BILLING & PAYMENT DRIVERS ---

Recharge Friction
  - Non-autopay customers must actively decide to pay every 28 days
  - Each recharge is a micro-churn-decision point
  - RDNI (Recharge Done, No Internet) is a SEVERE trust destroyer
    * Customer paid money but got nothing -> fraud perception
  - Cash-dependent customers have higher friction
  - PayG post-reset adds complexity: more frequent smaller payments

Grace Period Behavior
  - When plan expires, internet dies immediately (or after short grace)
  - For daily-wage earners, a 2-3 day grace period could be the
    difference between retention and permanent churn
  - Competing local cable operators often have flexible payment timing


2. BREAKAGE POINT MAP — CRITICAL MOMENTS THAT CREATE CHURN
======================================================================
Ranked by churn risk severity for Wiom's demographic:

#1. FIRST OUTAGE EXPERIENCE (Highest churn risk)
  Why: Sets expectation for all future service quality
  Customer experience: "Maine paise diye, internet nahi chala"
  Good handling: Proactive SMS/WhatsApp, quick fix, follow-up call
  Wiom risk: Partner may not have outage visibility or response SOP
  Intervention: Automated outage detection + customer notification

#2. REPEATED DISCONNECTION PATTERN (Cumulative)
  Why: Each disconnect is small but cumulative frustration is massive
  Customer experience: "Har ghante mein ek baar band ho jata hai"
  Threshold: After 10+ disconnects in a week, customer starts looking
  Wiom risk: WiFi infrastructure inherently less stable than fiber
  Intervention: Remote monitoring, proactive technician dispatch

#3. FIRST COMPLAINT RESOLUTION FAILURE
  Why: Creates permanent distrust in support system
  Customer experience: "Complaint kiya, koi aaya hi nahi"
  Recovery: If second attempt also fails, customer is mentally gone
  Wiom risk: Partner-based support lacks SLA enforcement
  Intervention: Escalation protocol, customer follow-up within 2 hrs

#4. RECHARGE DONE, NO INTERNET (RDNI)
  Why: Customer gave money and got nothing = perceived fraud
  Customer experience: "Paisa diya lekin net nahi chala"
  Severity: Even one instance can trigger immediate churn
  Wiom risk: System/sync issues between payment and activation
  Intervention: Real-time activation monitoring, instant refund SOP

#5. 28-DAY PLAN EXPIRY NOTIFICATION
  Why: Recurring reminder that Wiom charges more than calendar month
  Customer experience: "Abhi toh recharge kiya tha, phir se?"
  Cumulative effect: Compounds every month, mentioned by promoters too
  Wiom risk: Structural product issue, not just perception
  Intervention: Move to 30-day cycles or bridge with bonus days

#6. FIRST 48 HOURS POST-INSTALL
  Why: Speed doesn't match expectations -> immediate churn risk
  Customer experience: "Bola tha fast milega, slow aa raha hai"
  Good handling: Speed test at install, set realistic expectations
  Wiom risk: Partners may over-promise to close installation

#7. PRICE INCREASE OR PLAN CHANGE
  Why: Triggers value re-evaluation against alternatives
  Customer experience: "449 se 500 kar diya, kya mila extra?"
  Severity: High for price-sensitive base
  Wiom risk: Recent price changes (449->500) visible in NPS comments

#8. EXTENDED OUTAGE WITHOUT COMMUNICATION (>6 hours)
  Why: Customer feels abandoned, no information = worst experience
  Customer experience: "2 din se band hai, koi baat hi nahi karta"
  Good handling: Proactive updates every 2 hours during outage
  Wiom risk: Partner-level outage visibility may be limited

#9. COMPETITIVE OFFER EXPOSURE
  Why: JioFiber/Airtel marketing in neighborhood creates pull
  Customer experience: "Pados mein Jio laga, 30 din ka hai"
  Severity: Moderate (awareness -> consideration, not immediate churn)
  Wiom risk: Higher in Delhi where competitive density is highest

#10. ROUTER RETURN PRESSURE
  Why: Threatening behavior when customer delays recharge
  Customer experience: "Router wapas karo bol rahe hain"
  Severity: Moderate but creates negative word-of-mouth
  Wiom risk: Partners may use router return as pressure tactic


3. DELIGHT DRIVER MAP — WHAT CREATES PROMOTERS
======================================================================

#1. "IT JUST WORKS" — Invisible Reliability
  What: Internet is fast, stable, always on. Customer forgets about it.
  Why it delights: The best internet experience is one you never think about
  Evidence from NPS: "Acchi service" promoters often have NO specific
  complaint — absence of problems IS the delight
  Wiom lever: Partner uptime, infrastructure quality

#2. SPEED EXCEEDS EXPECTATION
  What: Customer expected slow (based on price), got fast
  Why it delights: Overdelivery on core promise creates trust
  Evidence: "Speed bahut acchi hai" — explicit speed praise
  Wiom lever: Under-promise, over-deliver on speed plans

#3. AFFORDABILITY CONFIRMATION
  What: Customer realizes they're getting genuine value for money
  Why it delights: Financial security + digital inclusion = powerful
  Evidence: "Sasta hai, koi extra charge nahi" — value validation
  Wiom lever: No hidden charges, transparent pricing, coupons

#4. FAST, EMPATHETIC SUPPORT
  What: Problem resolved quickly by someone who understands
  Why it delights: Recovery paradox — good recovery > no problem
  Evidence: "Turant solve kar dete hain" — immediate resolution
  Wiom lever: Partner training, SLA enforcement, follow-up

#5. EASY RECHARGE EXPERIENCE
  What: Multiple payment modes, no friction, works first time
  Why it delights: Reduces decision fatigue for frequent rechargers
  Evidence: Low effort score = higher NPS in recharge journey
  Wiom lever: UPI/autopay promotion, WhatsApp recharge option

#6. FREE WiFi ROAMING
  What: Can use any Wiom router nearby when home WiFi is down
  Why it delights: Unexpected perk, reduces downtime impact
  Evidence: Mentioned as differentiator by some promoters
  Wiom lever: Expand coverage density, promote awareness

#7. PROACTIVE COMMUNICATION
  What: Company tells you about issues before you notice
  Why it delights: Signals that company cares and is competent
  Evidence: Industry best practice, rarely seen in budget ISPs
  Wiom lever: Automated outage alerts via WhatsApp

#8. COUPONS & REWARDS
  What: Discount coupons, referral rewards, loyalty benefits
  Why it delights: Makes price-sensitive customers feel valued
  Evidence: "Coupons bhi milte hain" — mentioned by promoters
  Wiom lever: Coupon program already exists, needs amplification


4. LATENT DRIVERS — THINGS CUSTOMERS EXPERIENCE BUT DON'T ARTICULATE
======================================================================

These explain gaps between operational metrics and NPS scores:

4.1 DNS Resolution Speed
  - Pages load slow but speed test shows good bandwidth
  - Customer says "slow" but the real issue is DNS
  - NOT measured by Wiom (likely not even aware)
  - Impact: Moderate — affects perceived speed

4.2 Peak-Hour Throttling
  - Works fine at 2 AM, terrible at 8 PM
  - Partner-level bandwidth sharing means peak contention
  - Customer won't say "peak hour throttling" — says "slow"
  - Impact: HIGH — affects actual usage patterns

4.3 WiFi Interference
  - Neighbor's router on same channel, microwave, walls
  - Real issue is RF environment, not Wiom's service
  - But customer blames Wiom because it's their internet
  - Impact: Moderate — explains range/speed complaints

4.4 Device Limitations
  - Old smartphone can't utilize available bandwidth
  - Customer says "slow" but device is the bottleneck
  - Wiom can't control customer devices
  - Impact: Low-Moderate — creates unfair NPS penalty

4.5 Shared Bandwidth Within Home
  - 3-5 devices on one connection = everyone suffers
  - Family fights over bandwidth during peak
  - Customer says "slow" but issue is concurrent usage
  - Impact: Moderate — worsens with more devices

4.6 Router Placement
  - Router position determines WiFi quality throughout home
  - Bad placement -> range complaints, but customer won't
    articulate it as a placement issue
  - Impact: HIGH for range complaints — solvable at install

4.7 Power Supply Fluctuations
  - Unstable power -> router reboots -> "internet band ho gaya"
  - Common in Tier 2/3 areas and lower-income neighborhoods
  - Customer blames Wiom, but root cause is power quality
  - Impact: Moderate — explains some UP/Bharat churn differential

4.8 Weather Sensitivity
  - WiFi/wireless links degrade in heavy rain/storms
  - Seasonal NPS patterns may partially reflect weather
  - Customer says "server down" but issue is weather interference
  - Impact: Seasonal — explains periodic complaint spikes


5. COMPETITIVE CONTEXT
======================================================================

--- 5.1 Indian Home Internet Landscape (2025-26) ---

JioFiber
  - Fiber-to-home (FTTH) — inherently more reliable than WiFi
  - Rs 399/month for 30 Mbps, 30-day billing cycle
  - Strong brand equity, aggressive expansion into Tier 2
  - Bundled: 14+ OTT apps in higher plans
  - Advantage over Wiom: Reliability, brand, content
  - Disadvantage vs Wiom: Installation complexity, pricing inflexibility

Airtel Xstream Fiber
  - FTTH, premium positioning, Rs 499+ /month
  - Strongest customer support brand in India
  - Advantage: Support quality, reliability
  - Disadvantage vs Wiom: Price, not accessible everywhere

BSNL Fiber/Bharat Fiber
  - Government ISP, variable quality
  - Cheap but unreliable
  - Wiom competes in similar price range but better quality

Local Cable Operators
  - Hyper-local, cash-based, personal relationships
  - Often poor quality but flexible on payments
  - Wiom's direct competition in many neighborhoods
  - Advantage: Personal relationship with operator
  - Disadvantage: No app, no digital payments, inconsistent quality

--- 5.2 What This Means for NPS Interpretation ---

1. A 6/10 NPS might be EXCELLENT if local cable was the alternative
2. Delhi is highly competitive (JioFiber expanding) -> stricter NPS
3. UP/Bharat has fewer alternatives -> more tolerance for issues
4. "Affordable" driver may be masking quality tolerance
5. Low switching costs (no contract) mean every bad experience is
   a live churn risk — unlike contract ISPs with switching barriers
6. The 28-day billing is a REAL competitive disadvantage
   vs 30-day competitors — not just perception


6. CHALLENGE QUESTIONS FOR CX AND STATS TEAMS
======================================================================

For CX Team:
  Q1. Is uptime being measured at the right granularity? 95% monthly
      uptime can hide 6 hours of prime-time downtime.

  Q2. Are you measuring speed as the customer experiences it
      (device-level) or as the network delivers it (NAS-level)?
      These can differ by 50%+ due to WiFi conditions.

  Q3. How are you separating Wiom service issues from customer-side
      issues (bad device, bad power, bad router placement)?

  Q4. Is "complaint resolution" being measured from customer's
      perspective (first call to final resolution) or from system's
      perspective (ticket open to close)?

  Q5. Are you tracking REPEAT complaints? A customer who calls 3x
      for the same issue has a fundamentally different experience
      than one call, even if "resolution TAT" looks the same.

For Stats Team:
  Q6. Disconnection FREQUENCY vs DURATION — which actually predicts
      churn? A 5-second drop 10x/day may be worse than one 2-hour
      outage. Are you modeling both?

  Q7. How are you handling right-censoring bias? Sprint 13 (Jan '26)
      customers have had less time to churn than Sprint 1 (Jul '25).

  Q8. Is the 28-day billing cycle a DRIVER or a SYMPTOM of deeper
      value perception issues? Teasing apart is critical.

  Q9. Are you controlling for competitive density by geography?
      Delhi NPS should be evaluated differently than UP NPS.

  Q10. Response bias: Are commenting customers (42%) systematically
       different from non-commenting ones? If detractors comment
       more, theme analysis is biased toward problems.

For Both Teams:
  Q11. How are you handling PayG post-reset customers (post Jan 26)?
       Their recharge patterns are fundamentally different — higher
       frequency, lower amounts, shorter plans.

  Q12. What's your confidence that "General Good/Bad Service"
       (the two largest pre-coded categories) actually reflects
       distinct experiences? Or are these catch-all categories
       that mask heterogeneous issues?

  Q13. Partner quality varies enormously. Are you testing whether
       NPS variance WITHIN a partner's customer base is lower than
       BETWEEN partners? This would prove partner quality matters.

  Q14. Seasonal effects: Are July/August NPS scores different due
       to monsoon (more outages from rain) rather than service changes?

  Q15. The bimodal distribution (42% score 10, 20% score 0) —
       are these genuinely extreme experiences, or is this an artifact
       of survey design (most people default to extremes)?


7. WHAT WIOM SHOULD BE MEASURING BUT PROBABLY ISN'T
======================================================================

7.1 Customer-Premise Speed Tests
  Current: NAS-level throughput (infrastructure speed)
  Missing: Actual speed at customer device (WiFi conditions matter)
  Why it matters: 50%+ of "slow speed" complaints may be WiFi, not network

7.2 Peak-Hour Quality of Experience (QoE)
  Current: Average daily metrics
  Missing: 7-11 PM specific metrics
  Why it matters: All-day average masks the hours that matter most

7.3 First-Call Resolution Rate (FCR)
  Current: Ticket resolution TAT
  Missing: Was it resolved on first contact? How many follow-ups?
  Why it matters: FCR is the strongest predictor of support NPS globally

7.4 Touchpoint-Specific NPS
  Current: Overall NPS survey (single score)
  Missing: NPS at install, first complaint, recharge, outage recovery
  Why it matters: Isolates which touchpoint drives overall score

7.5 Time-to-Value (Post-Install)
  Current: Install TAT (booking to installation)
  Missing: How quickly customer starts meaningful usage after install
  Why it matters: Speed of habit formation predicts retention

7.6 Proactive Outage Detection Rate
  Current: Reactive (customer reports issue)
  Missing: % of outages detected before customer notices
  Why it matters: Proactive communication dramatically reduces NPS hit

7.7 WiFi Coverage Map Per Installation
  Current: None (install quality is subjective)
  Missing: Signal strength mapping at install
  Why it matters: Bad placement = chronic range complaints = detractor

7.8 Customer Device Health Monitoring
  Current: None
  Missing: Connected device types, capabilities, connection quality
  Why it matters: Old device + good network = "slow" complaint

7.9 Recharge Attempt Failure Rate
  Current: Successful recharges tracked
  Missing: Failed attempts (payment failed but customer tried)
  Why it matters: Failed recharge = frustration even if retry succeeds

7.10 Competitive Win/Loss Tracking
  Current: Churn flag (did they leave?)
  Missing: Where did they go? (JioFiber, Airtel, local cable?)
  Why it matters: Losing to JioFiber vs local cable = different strategy


8. TENURE-SPECIFIC INDUSTRY INSIGHTS
======================================================================

--- Onboarding (0-15 days) ---
  Primary driver: Installation quality and first speed impression
  Biggest churn risk: Speed doesn't match expectation
  Best intervention: Speed test at install, proactive day-3 check-in
  Industry insight: 60% of first-year churn decisions are made in
  the first 2 weeks (source: ISP industry benchmarks)

--- Early Life (16-60 days) ---
  Primary driver: Speed consistency and value realization
  Biggest churn risk: First complaint resolution failure
  Best intervention: Proactive outage communication, easy recharge
  Industry insight: Customers who survive 60 days without a major
  issue have 3x lower churn rate for the next 12 months

--- Establishing (61-120 days) ---
  Primary driver: Service reliability and support quality
  Biggest churn risk: Cumulative minor issues eroding trust
  Best intervention: Service quality monitoring, partner quality check
  Industry insight: This is when "habituation" happens — customer
  either builds internet into daily routine or starts looking

--- Steady State (121-270 days) ---
  Primary driver: Ongoing quality and competitive comparison
  Biggest churn risk: Competitive offer exposure + accumulated issues
  Best intervention: Loyalty recognition, competitive value messaging
  Industry insight: At this stage, passive satisfaction isn't enough —
  customer needs a reason to STAY, not just no reason to leave

--- Loyal (270+ days) ---
  Primary driver: Sustained quality, severe breakage avoidance
  Biggest churn risk: Major service failure after long period of trust
  Best intervention: VIP treatment, proactive issue prevention
  Industry insight: Loyal customers have HIGHER expectations and
  react MORE negatively to failures (expectation gap is wider).
  A loyal customer's bad NPS score is a louder alarm than a new
  customer's bad score.
  NOTE: 96.6% of NPS respondents fall here — this IS the analysis.


9. KEY INDEPENDENT FINDINGS & RECOMMENDATIONS
======================================================================

FINDING 1: The 28-Day Billing Cycle Is a Strategic Vulnerability
  Not just a complaint theme — it's a structural competitive
  disadvantage. Every month, customers are reminded they pay more
  per day than JioFiber/Airtel customers. This compounds over time
  and is mentioned even by PROMOTERS. Fix this or it will bleed
  customers as competitor coverage expands.

FINDING 2: Connection Stability > Speed for This Segment
  Wiom's customers need sufficient speed (10-25 Mbps), not maximum
  speed. But they need it CONSISTENTLY. Frequent disconnections
  are likely the top NPS driver, ahead of speed. Invest in
  stability over raw throughput.

FINDING 3: The Partner IS the Brand
  For Wiom's customers, the partner (Rohit) is the company. Bad
  partner = bad Wiom, regardless of network quality. Partner
  quality clustering should be a PRIMARY feature in churn models.
  The variance between partners is likely larger than the variance
  between geographies.

FINDING 4: Proactive Communication Is a Missing Competitive Advantage
  Budget ISPs almost never communicate proactively. If Wiom can
  send WhatsApp alerts during outages and before plan expiry,
  this alone could move NPS 5-10 points for affected customers.

FINDING 5: PayG Post-Reset Customers Are a Different Segment
  Post Jan 26 PayG customers have fundamentally different economics:
  - Smaller, more frequent recharges
  - Lower commitment signal per recharge
  - More churn-decision points per month
  - Different recharge frequency != different tenure
  These MUST be modeled separately or with a PayG interaction flag.

FINDING 6: Right-Censoring Will Bias Churn Models
  Sprint 13 and Sprint 14 customers haven't had enough time to
  churn. Sprint RSP1-3 (Feb '26) even less. Unless survival
  analysis or time-windowed churn definitions are used, recent
  sprints will artificially appear to have lower churn.

FINDING 7: The Bimodal Distribution Suggests Survey Design Issues
  42.6% scoring 10 and 20.4% scoring 0 suggests either:
  a) Genuinely extreme experiences (possible for budget ISP), or
  b) Survey design pushing to extremes (no explanation of scale)
  c) Cultural tendency to express in extremes (very Hindi-culture)
  This affects whether we should model NPS as continuous or
  as a 3-group classification (Promoter/Passive/Detractor).
