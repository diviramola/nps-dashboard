  THREE KEY INSIGHTS FROM THE CLEAN MODEL:

  INSIGHT 1: OUTBOUND CALLS IS THE #1 CHURN PREDICTOR
  Customers who receive more outbound calls from Wiom are less likely to
  churn. This could mean: (a) proactive outreach genuinely retains customers,
  or (b) the retention team targets at-risk customers who then stay.
  Either way, it validates investment in proactive customer contact.
  ACTION: Scale proactive outreach programs; track outbound call → retention
  conversion rates to establish causal direction.

  INSIGHT 2: RELIABILITY > SPEED
  avg_uptime_pct (#2) and stddev_uptime (#10) are strong churn predictors.
  Speed gap (sc_speed_gap_pct) and speed-related features barely register.
  Customers tolerate speed variation but NOT connection drops.
  ACTION: Prioritize network reliability and uptime monitoring over
  speed upgrades. A stable 50 Mbps connection retains better than an
  unstable 100 Mbps connection.

  INSIGHT 3: TICKET RESOLUTION QUALITY >> TICKET VOLUME
  SLA compliance (#3), resolution time (#4, #6), and overall SLA (#8)
  cluster in the top 10. Total ticket count (#5) matters, but HOW
  tickets are resolved matters more than how many there are.
  ACTION: Invest in resolution speed and first-contact resolution
  rates, not just ticket deflection strategies.

  - complaint_resolution_bad: 85.1% Detractors, NPS 2.4

  INDUSTRY EXPERT VIEW
  - Independent finding #3: "The Partner IS the Brand"
  - Partner quality variance > geography variance
  - "A bad partner = bad Wiom, regardless of network quality"
  - This is a known pattern in last-mile ISPs: the field tech shapes
    the ENTIRE perception of the service

  RECOMMENDED ACTION
  1. Build Partner Quality Composite Score:
     - Partner uptime % (30-day avg)
     - Median install TAT
     - Ticket SLA compliance rate (tk_sla_compliance — churn predictor #3)
     - Average NPS of their customers
  2. Tier partners A/B/C based on composite score
  3. A-tier: higher commissions, priority install allocation
  4. C-tier: mandatory training, 90-day improvement window, supervised
  5. Auto-escalate any complaint from C-tier partner customers

  EXPECTED IMPACT: Moving bottom-quartile partners to median could shift
  15-20% of their Detractors toward Passive/Promoter. Estimated NPS
  improvement: +3 to +5 points within 2 sprints of implementation.

  THIS SECTION IS NEW IN v2. Outbound calls was not highlighted in v1

  the model. With those removed, OUTBOUND_CALLS emerges as #1.

  EVIDENCE STRENGTH: MEDIUM-HIGH (strong quant, moderate qual/industry)

  QUANTITATIVE (Churn)
  - OUTBOUND_CALLS: #1 churn predictor (importance 0.084)
  - MISSED_CALLS: #16 (importance 0.020)
  - TOTAL_IVR_CALLS: #15 (importance 0.021)
  - The call interaction cluster accounts for ~12.5% of total model
    predictive power — the largest single category.

  QUALITATIVE
  - call_center_bad theme: 21 comments, 38.1% churn
  - Customer voice: "customer care call nahi uthata"
    (customer care doesn't answer calls)
  - Low comment volume but high churn when mentioned negatively

  INTERPRETATION — TWO POSSIBLE MECHANISMS:
  A) PROACTIVE OUTREACH RETAINS: Customers who receive more outbound
     calls feel cared for and are less likely to churn. This supports
     investing in proactive check-in programs.
  B) RETENTION TARGETING WORKS: The existing retention team may already
     be calling at-risk customers, and some of those customers stay.
     This validates the effectiveness of targeted retention efforts.

  In either case, the signal is clear: MORE PROACTIVE CONTACT = LESS CHURN.

  RECOMMENDED ACTION
  1. Scale proactive outbound call program:
     - Day 3: New customer check-in ("How's your internet working?")
     - Day 15: First-month satisfaction check
     - Post-complaint: Follow-up within 2 hours
     - Risk-triggered: Call when operational metrics show degradation
  2. Track outbound call → retention conversion rates
  3. Ensure outbound calls are HELPFUL not HARASSING:
     - Brief, purpose-driven calls (not upselling)
     - WhatsApp as alternative to phone calls
     - Respect customer communication preferences
  4. Target: 100% of Detractors contacted within 48 hours of NPS survey

  EXPECTED IMPACT: If proactive outreach can shift 10% of at-risk
  customers from churning to staying, that's ~300 saved customers
  per sprint (10% of 3,057 churners).

  7. DRIVER DEEP-DIVE #5: 28-DAY BILLING CYCLE
     "A self-inflicted competitive wound"
============================================================================

  EVIDENCE STRENGTH: MEDIUM-HIGH (qual + industry strong; quant indirect)

  THE MATH CUSTOMERS DO
  - 28-day cycle = 13 billing cycles per year
  - Competitors (JioFiber, Airtel) use 30-day = 12 cycles per year
  - Extra cycle cost: Rs 500-600/year at typical plan prices
  - For a Rs 399/month customer, that's 8.3% more expensive annually

  QUALITATIVE EVIDENCE
  - billing_28day theme: 57 comments, 73.7% Detractors, NPS 3.0
  - Churn rate: 31.6% (4th highest of any theme)
  - Even PROMOTERS complain about it — Kano Reverse Quality
  - The irritation COMPOUNDS with tenure: 69.8% from 6+ month bucket
  - Customer voice: "Ye to upbhoktaon ke saath seedha dhokha hai"
    (This is outright fraud against consumers)

  INDUSTRY EXPERT VIEW
  - Finding #1: "Strategic Vulnerability"
  - "Recurring reminder that Wiom charges more than competitors"
  - Every recharge is a moment where the customer does competitive math
  - This is the EASIEST competitor talking point: "We charge monthly,
    they charge every 28 days"

  RECOMMENDED ACTION
  Option A: Move to 30-day billing cycle (clean, permanent fix)
  Option B: Add 2 "bonus days" per cycle for 6+ month customers
  Revenue impact: ~3.7% fewer recharge events/year (13 -> 12.2)
  Offset: reduced churn + eliminated competitive disadvantage

  EXPECTED IMPACT: Eliminates a Reverse Quality entirely. Could prevent
  18-30 churns per sprint among billing-sensitive customers.

  EVIDENCE STRENGTH: MEDIUM-HIGH (qual + industry strong; quant moderate)

  THE BAD NEWS: PRICE SENSITIVITY IS A DOUBLE-EDGED SWORD
  - pricing_expensive theme: NPS 5.3, churn 28.9%
  - Customers who feel overcharged churn at 4.5x the rate of those
    who feel Wiom is affordable (28.9% vs 6.4%)
  - The 28-day billing cycle erodes the affordability perception

  INDUSTRY EXPERT VIEW
  - Ranked #3 in ISP driver taxonomy (value perception)
  - "Price-to-quality ratio vs alternatives is the key metric"
  - Low switching costs amplify price sensitivity in this segment
  - JTBD: Wiom wins on "affordable internet for children's education"

  RECOMMENDED ACTION
  1. DO NOT increase prices without proportional value addition
  2. Address 28-day perception (see Driver #5)
  3. Transparent pricing communication at onboarding
  4. Communicate total value (speed + reliability + support) not just price
  5. Monitor competitor pricing in each geography monthly

  EXPECTED IMPACT: Protecting the affordability moat maintains Wiom's
  primary competitive advantage. Price increases without visible value
  improvements would trigger outsized churn in this price-sensitive base.

  3. Outage data covers only ~20 days (Dec 20 - Jan 8, 2026)
  4. Only 42% of respondents wrote comments (selection bias)
  5. Outbound calls: correlation, not proven causation (could be
     retention team targeting at-risk customers who would have stayed anyway)
  6. Install TAT analysis has insufficient sample for new customers
     (only 25 in 0-15 day bucket)
  7. Competitive context is not in the data (no neighborhood-level)
  8. Promoter churn (20.2%) is abnormally high and partially unexplained

  C. METHODOLOGY SUMMARY
  - Phase 0: 13,045 NPS responses parsed from Excel, joined to Snowflake
  - Phase 1: 5,488 Hindi/Hinglish/English comments processed through
    NLP pipeline (cleaning, transliteration, translation, sentiment)
    23 emergent themes discovered via unsupervised clustering
  - Phase 2: CX frameworks applied (SERVQUAL, Kano, CES, JTBD)
  - Phase 2b: Independent ISP industry expert assessment
  - Phase 3: 298 features engineered from Snowflake operational data

  - Phase 4: Key Driver Analysis (4 methods: Ridge, Random Forest,

  - Phase 4B v3: Leakage-free churn model (AUC 0.9414, 5-fold CV),

  - Phase 5: Triangulation of qualitative, quantitative, and industry

  - Phase 6: This leadership synthesis (v2)

  D. DATA LEAKAGE CONTROLS (EXPANDED in v2)

  The v1 churn model (AUC 0.977) suffered from multiple forms of data
  leakage that inflated its apparent accuracy. The v3 model addresses
  all identified leakage sources:

  1. CHURN-DERIVED FEATURES (computed FROM the churn label):
     Excluded: churn_risk_score, churn_risk_pct, churn_label,
     risk_category, partner_churn_rate, partner_at_risk,
     partner_risk_level

  2. PAYMENT/RECHARGE FEATURES (mechanically = churn definition,
     since Wiom churn = 16 days no recharge):
     Excluded: total_payments, PAYMENT_ATTEMPTS, PAYMENT_SUCCESSES,
     avg_payment_amount, total_recharges, days_since_last_recharge,
     recharge_regularity, and 15+ additional payment-related features

  3. DATA-EXISTENCE PROXIES (features that measure how long a
     customer's data exists in the system, which correlates with
     active status):
     Excluded: sc_scorecard_weeks, cis_influx_data_days,
     ul1_usage_data_days, sc_plan_active_ratio, cis_active_day_ratio

  4. FILL-RATE LEAKAGE (features with dramatically different data
     availability for Active vs Churned customers):
     Addressed via two-track approach:
     - Track 1: Only features with <10pp fill-rate gap (145 features)
     - Track 2: All features but only on rows with complete data
       (no imputation needed, N=9,664)

  5. ACTIVITY STATUS FEATURES:
     Excluded: partner_status, partner_status_at_survey, call_status

  Result: AUC dropped from 0.977 (leaky) to 0.9414 (clean).
  This is a MORE TRUSTWORTHY result. An AUC of 0.94 is still excellent
  for customer churn prediction and indicates that operational metrics
  genuinely capture churn risk.

  NPS: +0.0 | Promoters: 46% | Detractors: 46% | Churn: 23%

  ROOT CAUSE: Wiom's NPS is dragged down by human delivery and process

  TOP 6 DRIVERS               ACTION                       TIMELINE
  -------------------------   ---------------------------   ---------
  1. Partner/Rohit Quality    Quality tiering + training    0-12 wks
  2. Complaint Handling       Service recovery SOP          0-6 wks

  THE CHURN MODEL FINDING (v3, leakage-free, AUC 0.9414):
  The top 10 churn predictors are ALL operational metrics that Wiom
  can directly improve: outbound calls, uptime, SLA compliance,
  resolution speed, and ticket quality. NPS score adds ZERO lift.
  This means: invest in OPERATIONAL MONITORING, not survey scores.

  RELIABILITY > SPEED: Network uptime (#2 predictor) vastly outweighs
  speed metrics. Customers tolerate speed variation but NOT disconnections.
  Prioritize stable connections over faster connections.

  RESOLUTION QUALITY > TICKET VOLUME: Five of the top 10 churn
  predictors are ticket/resolution metrics. How problems are solved
  matters far more than how many problems occur. Invest in resolution
  speed and first-contact resolution, not ticket deflection.

  PROACTIVE OUTREACH WORKS: Outbound calls are the #1 churn predictor.
  Customers who receive proactive contact are significantly less likely
  to churn. Scale the outbound program and track retention conversion.

  HIGHEST-ROI QUICK WIN: Implement service recovery SOP (2-hour
  follow-up + repeat complaint alerts). 6.8-point NPS gap between bad
  and good complaint resolution. Achievable in 2-4 weeks.

  BIGGEST STRUCTURAL FIX: Move to 30-day billing cycle. Eliminates a
  self-inflicted competitive disadvantage that compounds with tenure
  and creates 31.6% churn among affected customers.

  DATA GAPS TO CLOSE: Deploy device-level speed and uptime monitoring.
  The two biggest customer complaints (speed, disconnections) are
  underserved by current infrastructure-level metrics. Closing this gap
  would transform Wiom's ability to identify and fix individual issues.
